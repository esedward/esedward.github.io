About Me

I graduated from the University of Waterloo Computer Science (BCS) program in 2022 with a specialization in Computational Fine Arts and a minor in French Studies. My interests lie at the intersection of creativity and technology, whether that's thinking outside the box to solve interesting problems or creating beautiful things with code. When I'm not in front of my computer you can find me nose deep in a book or experimenting in the kitchen. I'm currently looking for full-time opportunities where I can be part of a dynamic team and continue my growth as a software developer.

This non-recursive ray tracer was written in C++ and is capable of secondary shadow rays and surface intersection with geometric primitives and triangle meshes. I implemented specular shading using the Lambertian model and anti-aliasing by super sampling. I explored topics in light and shading models, lenses, and surface intersection with quartic functions.

This piece was inspired by my friend's research for her Masters in Genetics. Written in Processing, the circular agents moving along the screen represent flies under a microscope, reproducing and passing on their genetic traits.

Visual Weather Report

This piece was inspired by the weather animations on mobile devices. Written in Processing, the code determines the high and the low temperature for the current day and uses this information to create a background gradient with colours based on temperature. It also looks at the precipitation status and the wind direction and speed.

This DEMO was created for a Human-Computer Interaction course using Android Studio in collaboration with Kayla Hardie, Kevin Lu, and Kriti Ravindran. We created a high-fidelity prototype for an app that recommends recipes to reduce food waste and interviewed target users to test user-experience.
